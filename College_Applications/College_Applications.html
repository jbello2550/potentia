College Applications

General College Application Information

There are many different ways for students to apply to college. The most common method students use is the Common App. The Common App is a host for more than 750 schools, in and out of the US. Even though Common App is widely accepted, some schools do not accept the Common App, for example, Georgetown University, University of Texas-Austin, some Ivy League schools as well as all 9 University of California locations. The Common App is not connected to all colleges but most institutions use common app to go through applications.

What is Common App?

The Common App helps students to apply to multiple colleges at once, research financial aid and scholarships, as well as connect to college counseling resources. Common App lowers logistical as well as systemic barriers to help students from a multitude of backgrounds apply to college.

Dates and Deadlines

  • First deadlines to come up are early decision deadlines. Early decision deadlines are normally sometime in November. Early decision applications are when students who apply early normally receive their letters from college earlier than students who apply during regular application submission. When a student is accepted through the early decision process, it is almost always required that they enroll into the institution. Students who apply for early decision normally hear back in January or early February.
  • Students who apply to regular decision generally hears back from schools in mid-to-late March or April.
  • Another type of admission some schools use rolling admissions. Rolling admissions evaluate applications as the admissions office receives them. These schools also tend to release decisions on a regular basis and have no hard cut-off date for applications.
