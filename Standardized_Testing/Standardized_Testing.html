What is it?

There are three types of standardized tests that students take when applying to college: the ACT, the SAT, and the SAT Subject Tests. The ACT and SAT test students' ability to understand and present ideas and information. The SAT Subject Tests, on the other hand, deal with specific knowledge on certain topics such as biology or history. While schools are required to accept both SAT and ACT tests in admission, schools will often require taking specified SAT Subject Tests for different courses. It is important to know what and where your child wants to study to ensure that all required tests are taken.

AP
Advanced Placement, or AP, is a program run by the College Board that allows highschool students to experience classes on par with intro-level college courses. Students in AP have the opportunity to earn college credit, enabling them to take more advanced classes when they start college. College credit is only given to students based on if they pass the AP exam and if their college accepts that AP credit. The AP exam is scored on a scale of 1-to-5, on which 3 is considered passing. Taking AP classes can improve a student’s college application by showing that the AP class they took was something they cared about, as well as showing that they can manage the rigor involved with such a course.

  1. Go to the College Board website's SAT registration page
  2. Click the “Register Now” button
